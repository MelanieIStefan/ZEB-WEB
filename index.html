About

ZEB is the Zhejiang-Edinburgh Endocrinology and Brain Group. We are a group of researchers associated with the (ZJU-UOE Institute) with an interest in endocrinology and neuroscience.

Keywords

Stress; HPA axis; pregnancy; behaviour; neurosteroids; oxytocin

Reasearch Interests

My group investigates the long-term neurobiological, endocrine and behavioural impact of early life stress on subsequent health and well-being. Using the rat as a model, we investigate the effects of stress exposure during pregnancy on the mother, the pregnancy and her offspring. Our goal is to better understand: (i) how stress negatively affects the pregnancy, the mother and her offspring and the underlying mechanisms; (ii) how the adverse effects of stress are transmitted from the mother to the fetus and; (iii) how the effects can be prevented or reversed.

Expertise

In vivo rodent experiments; stress models; stereotaxic surgery; microdialysis; blood sampling (in conscious freely moving rats); behavioural assessments (e.g. anxiety, depression, cognition); peptide/steroid hormone measurements; in situ hybridisation; immunocytochemistry.

John Menzies

Centre for Discovery Brain Sciences, University of Edinburgh, Edinburgh, UK

Website

Keywords

Appetite; food choice; behaviour; gut-brain axis; hypothalamus; oxytocin

Reasearch Interests

I am interested in how nutritional and hormonal signals are integrated by the brain to produce relevant behaviours. I focus on hypothalamic oxytocin neurones, how these are modulated by energy balance-related signals, and how dendritically-relased oxytocin acts in the brain. The aim is to better understand how interactions between long- and short-term signals, and their actions of specific brain targets, influence food choice behaviour. I also use rat models of positive mood to study the effects of mood on eating and food choice behaviours.

Expertise

in vivo rodent studies; mood-, eating- and food choice-related behaviours; opto- and chemogenetics; immunocytochemistry and neuronal tracing; in vitro electrophysiology.

Reasearch Interests

I am currently researching the mechanisms by which steroid hormones such as androgens and estrogens act in the hypothalamus and pituitary to control male reproduction and testosterone production. I am also interested in improving the way that biological pathways are depicted as diagrams and have been involved in developing the modified Edinburgh Pathway Notation (mEPN) for use as a dynamic modelling system.

Expertise

Analysis of reproductive parameters, breeding and maintenance of complex transgenic mouse lines, histology and immunohistochemistry, peptide/steroid hormone measurements, qPCR and gene expression analysis, modelling the structure and dynamics of biological pathways.

Nicola Romano

Centre for Discovery Brain Sciences, University of Edinburgh, Edinburgh, UK

Website

Keywords

Hormonal rhythms; pituitary gland; hypothalamus; stress; prolactin; pathway dynamics and modelling

Reasearch Interests

I am interested in understanding how the brain controls the production of hormones from the pituitary gland. Specifically, how do different patterns of signals from the brain differentially affect intracellular processes in pituitary cells? How does the pituitary process and integrate the information it receives from the brain?

I am also interested in studying how communication between cells affects their behaviour: in particular, the role of the 3D structure of the pituitary for the interpretation of external signals is still one of the most unclear yet fascinating areas in the neuroendocrine field.

Presently I am working on regulation of intracellular pathways in ACTH producing cells (corticotrophs), which control the endocrine responses to stress. In the past most of my research has been centred on hormones that control reproduction, such as GnRH, controlling fertility and prolactin, controlling lactation and maternal behaviour.

Expertise

live imaging (calcium and intracellular pathways) in cells and tissue slices; luminometry; immunohistochemistry and 3D image analysis; (big) data analysis;
